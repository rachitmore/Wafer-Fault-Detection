So, who am I?

My name is Rachit More, and my journey has been a blend of engineering precision and data-driven exploration. I graduated with a Bachelor of Engineering in Mechanical Engineering in 2020. Fresh out of college, I embarked on my professional journey as a Quality Control In-charge at Confidence Petroleum India Limited. This role, which I held for a year and a half, taught me the importance of meticulousness and quality assurance.

Seeking to broaden my horizons, I moved on to Safecare Gasoline Private Limited, where I took on dual roles as a Project Engineer and continued my responsibilities in Quality Control. This period of my career was not just about managing projects and ensuring quality; it was a time of exploration. I found myself increasingly fascinated by the emerging trends and technologies that were shaping the future, particularly Data Science.

The more I learned about Data Science, the more I realized its potential and the impact it could have on various industries. This realization sparked a passion in me that I could not ignore. I made the bold decision to leave my stable job and immerse myself in the world of Data Science, starting from the very basics. I enrolled in several comprehensive courses to build a strong foundation: Full Stack Data Science from iNeuron, Statistics for Data Science and Business from 360DataScience, and Python for Data Science and Machine Learning from Jose Portila.

These courses were just the beginning. I interned at iNeuron, where I had the opportunity to work on open-source projects under the guidance of seasoned mentors. This hands-on experience was invaluable and helped me apply theoretical knowledge to real-world problems. After my internship, I began taking on freelance projects, allowing me to further refine my skills and gain diverse experiences in the field.

Throughout this journey, I have learned that following one's passion requires courage and a willingness to start anew. My transition from mechanical engineering to Data Science has been challenging yet incredibly rewarding. I am excited about the future and eager to continue growing and contributing to this dynamic field.
